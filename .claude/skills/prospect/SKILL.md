# Prospect — Client Acquisition Intelligence

Use when building lead lists, writing outreach, or qualifying inbound leads.

## When to Apply

- Building a prospect list for a new service-business niche
- Writing the first message to a cold lead
- Following up on a lead who hasn't responded
- Qualifying an inbound inquiry to decide how to respond

## Workflows

### 1. Build Prospect List
Given: service type + city/region + target count

Steps:
1. Define search strings for Google Maps and paslaugos.lt (Lithuanian market)
2. For each prospect, capture: Business name | Phone | Website (Y/N) | Reviews | Last active
3. Score each 1–3: 3 = no website or terrible one + active reviews = hot lead
4. Output markdown table sorted by score descending
5. Flag top 5 as "start here"

Website-less businesses with active Google reviews = highest priority. They have proven demand but zero digital infrastructure.

### 2. Cold Outreach Message
Given: prospect name, service type, specific observation about their business

Framework — 4 lines max:
1. One specific observation (not a compliment — something real: "I noticed you have 47 reviews but no website")
2. One concrete outcome ("businesses like yours with a site convert 3x more calls from Google")
3. One zero-friction offer ("I can show you a 10-min mockup for free, no commitment")
4. One CTA ("Worth a quick call this week?")

Channel defaults:
- Facebook Messenger: most responsive for Lithuanian small businesses
- SMS: highest open rate, use for warm leads only
- Email: use if they have a listed email and seem more formal

Never: generic compliments, long intros, "I hope this message finds you well", multiple questions in one message.

### 3. Follow-up Sequence
- Day 0: first message (outreach above)
- Day 4: one-line bump — just add value ("saw a competitor of yours just launched a site, thought of you")
- Day 10: soft close ("I'm onboarding 2 new clients this month — happy to save a spot if timing works")
- After that: move to a low-priority list, check back in 6 weeks

### 4. Qualify Inbound Lead
Given: an inquiry message

Score on 4 factors (1–3 each):
- Budget signal: mentions price? sounds price-sensitive?
- Decision speed: urgent need vs. just browsing
- Fit: is this a service you actually do?
- Location: within your region?

Total 10–12 = respond fast, prioritise. 4–6 = respond normally. Under 4 = polite pass or referral.

## Output Principles
- All outreach copy: write in the voice of the sender (informal LT for local clients, professional EN for international)
- Prospect tables: always include a "Why now" column (specific reason to contact them today)
- Never fabricate review counts or business details — mark unknowns as "?"
